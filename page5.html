Vegetables & Fruits

Most people should aim at least 9 servings ( at least 4 cups) of vegetables and fruits a day, and for the potatoes it shouldn’t count. 

Cholesterol & Fats

The total amount of fat t you eat, whether high or low isn’t really linked with disease. The important is to limit the amount of cholesterol you eat. The biggest influence on blood cholesterol level is the mix of fats in your diet not the amount of cholesterol you eat from foods. 

Fiber

Fiber is a type of carbohydrate that the body can’t digest. Most adult women should shoot for over 20 grams of fibers a day. Men should shoot for over 30 grams per a day. Great sources are whole fruits and vegetables, whole grain breads and breakfast cereals and all manner of beans. 

Carbohydrates

Don’t be misled by fad diets that make blanket pronouncements on the dangers of carbohydrates. They provide the body with fuel it needs for physical activity and for proper organ function. 

Protein

When choosing protein rich foods pay attention to what comes along with the protein. The best animal protein choices are fish and poultry. 

Calcium & Milk

Calcium and dairy can lower the risk of osteoporosis and colon cancer. Dairy products can be higher in saturated fat as well as retinol (vitamin A) which at high levels can paradoxically weaken bones. 

Healthy diet refers to nutrition that encompasses a wide range of food groups taken by an individual in the amount recommended in accordance with the needs of a person's body. About 3 groups which is children, adults, and seniors each of these individual groups require different amounts of macronutrients and micronutrients.

 Macronutrient refers to the food components required in large quantities of carbohydrates, proteins and fats that will be counted as calories. Micronutrients also refer to components such as vitamins and minerals that are needed by the human body in a small quantity but important to ensure that the human body system is functioning properly.

 According to Recommended Nutrient Intakes for Malaysia 2017 (RNI 2017), boys aged 4 to 9 require 1300-70 calories per day while girls aged 4 to 9 years need 1210-1810 calories per day. Adult mans need 1960-2470 calories per day while adult women need 1610-2130 calories per day. Citizens aged 60 and above need 1780-2280 calories for men and 1550-1990 calories for women. It should be noted that the total calorie requirement for any group or individual depends on the level of physical activity either less active, moderately active, or highly active.

 Of course, healthy eating habits should be nurtured starting from home again by parents and their families. This is because unhealthy eating habits will lead to excessive weight problems during childhood and will continue to adulthood if these children are not given proper exposure to healthy eating.

 Nowadays Malaysia often facing an increase in the number of overweight children. According to the National Health and Morbidity Survey study conducted in 2015 or more commonly known as the National Health and Morbidity Survey 2015, the percentage of children aged 18 years and under who have overweight problems of 11.9% compared to 6-8 % experienced worldwide. Children should be given guidance and exposure to healthy nutrition such as food selection at school canteens, healthy snack selection and so on.
